# Product Training Guide

## Overview

Final expense life insurance is a simplified-issue whole life product designed for seniors who want to cover burial and end-of-life costs without a medical exam. This guide covers product types, key policy features, common riders, and how to position each product to a specific prospect.

---

## What Is Final Expense Insurance?

Final expense (FE) insurance is whole life insurance with:
- **Small face amounts** — typically $2,000 to $40,000
- **No medical exam** — approval based on health questions only
- **Guaranteed level premiums** — never increase
- **Guaranteed cash value** — builds over time, never decreases
- **Guaranteed death benefit** — never decreases (on level plans)
- **Permanent coverage** — does not expire at age 65, 70, or any age

It is **not** term insurance. It does not expire. This is one of the most important distinctions to make with prospects.

## Policy Types

### Level Benefit (Preferred)
- Full death benefit payable from day one
- Lowest premium for a given face amount
- Requires health qualification
- Ideal for: healthy to moderately healthy seniors

**Selling Point:**
> "From the moment this policy is issued, if something happens to you, your family gets the full [face amount]. No waiting period."

---

### Graded Benefit
- Full benefit available after a waiting period (typically 2 years)
- Years 1–2: usually returns 110% of premiums paid upon death
- Year 3+: full death benefit
- Higher premium than level
- For: prospects who don't qualify for level due to moderate health issues

**How to Present:**
> "This plan works a little differently in the first couple of years. If something were to happen, your family would receive all the premiums you paid back plus 10 percent. After 24 months, they receive the full [face amount]. A lot of my clients in similar situations choose this because it's still real protection."

---

### Modified Benefit
- Years 1–3: return of premium + interest (typically 5–10%)
- Year 4+: full death benefit
- Higher premium
- For: prospects with significant health issues who still qualify

### Guaranteed Issue (GI)
- No health questions — everyone is accepted
- 2-year waiting period (accidental death covered immediately at most carriers)
- Highest premium for a given face amount
- For: prospects who cannot qualify for any other plan

**Positioning:**
> "No matter what your health situation is, you are guaranteed to be approved. There are no health questions. The only condition is that your beneficiary would receive the premiums back if something happened in the first two years — but after that, the full benefit is paid."

## Face Amount Selection

### The Average Funeral Cost Anchor
> "The average funeral today costs between $10,000 and $15,000 when you add up the casket, burial plot, flowers, obituary, and reception. Most of my clients want to at minimum cover that."

### Stacking Coverage
If a prospect already has some coverage:
> "So you have $5,000 with AARP. If we add a $10,000 plan here, your family would have $15,000 total — which is right in line with what funerals cost today."

## Key Policy Features to Explain

### 1. Premiums Never Increase
> "Once this policy is issued, your premium is locked in for life. It will never go up — not next year, not in 20 years."

### 2. Coverage Never Expires
> "This is not a term policy that runs out when you turn 80. This is whole life — it stays in force as long as you pay the premium."

### 3. Cash Value Accumulation
> "Over time, this policy builds cash value — kind of like a savings component. You can borrow against it if you ever need to."

## Competitor Comparison

### vs. AARP / New York Life
- AARP rates **increase with age** — FE rates are locked
- AARP coverage is term-based and can increase in cost significantly
- FE is whole life — never expires

**Script:**
> "The plan you have through AARP is actually set up so the premium goes up every 5 years as you get older. The plan I'm showing you today locks your premium in permanently — it will never go up, and it will never run out."

### vs. Colonial Penn $9.95
- Colonial Penn sells units, not a fixed face amount
- A $9.95/month "unit" may only provide $800–$1,000 in coverage at age 70
- FE provides a guaranteed, named face amount

**Script:**
> "The commercials make it sound like you get a full policy for $9.95, but what you actually get is what they call a 'unit' — and at your age, that unit might only be worth $800 or so in coverage. I want to show you what a guaranteed [$X] in coverage actually costs."

### vs. Globe Life
- Globe Life's initial rate is artificially low and increases
- Coverage caps are low
- Full underwriting required at higher amounts

## Positioning by Prospect Scenario

| Scenario | Recommended Approach |
|---|---|
| Healthy, budget-conscious | Lead with Mutual of Omaha; compare daily cost |
| Diabetic, otherwise healthy | Lead with Americo Eagle; mention diabetes specialty |

| Cancer survivor (5+ yrs) | Lead with Foresters; mention fraternal benefits |

| Uninsurable | Guaranteed issue — emphasize guaranteed acceptance |
