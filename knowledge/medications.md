# Medication Reference Guide

## Overview

Medications reveal underlying conditions that prospects may not volunteer. During a call, asking about medications is often a softer entry point than asking about diagnoses directly. This file maps common medications to the conditions they indicate and notes the underwriting implications.

> **Disclaimer:** This guide is for field reference only. Never diagnose, advise, or comment on a prospect's medication regimen. Use medication information solely to anticipate underwriting classification.

---

## How to Ask About Medications

> "Are you currently taking any prescription medications? I ask because some of our plans are more favorable depending on what someone is being treated for, and I want to find you the best fit."

If they list medications, follow up:
> "And those are all to manage [condition]? Are there any other conditions you take medication for?"

## Chemotherapy / Oncology Medications

If a prospect mentions any oncology medication, immediately clarify:

> "Are you currently going through any cancer treatment, or is that something you completed in the past?"

- **Currently in treatment** → Guaranteed Issue only
- **Completed treatment** → Probe for cancer type and years in remission; consider Foresters (5+ years)

## Medication Red Flags

If a prospect mentions any of the following, escalate to GI discussion:

- [ ] Dialysis medications (sevelamer, cinacalcet, epoetin) → on dialysis
- [ ] Hospice medications (morphine continuous infusion, comfort meds) → terminal
- [ ] High-dose immunosuppressants post-transplant → organ transplant
- [ ] Active chemotherapy agents → currently in cancer treatment

---

## How to Handle "I Don't Know What It's For"

Many seniors take medications without knowing their purpose. Stay calm and probe:

> "That's okay — do you have the bottle nearby? You can read me the name and I can look it up for you."

Or:
> "No worries at all. Can you tell me what health conditions your doctor has diagnosed you with? We can work from that direction instead."
