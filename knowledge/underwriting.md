# Underwriting Reference Guide

## Overview

Underwriting determines whether a prospect qualifies for a level, graded, modified, or guaranteed issue policy. Gathering accurate health information during the call saves time and prevents declined applications. This guide covers the key health questions, what the answers mean, and how to guide the conversation.

### 2. Tobacco Use
"Tobacco user" is defined differently across carriers — some include nicotine patches and chewing tobacco; others only count cigarettes. Always clarify.

**How to ask:**
> "Are you a tobacco user — cigarettes, cigars, chewing tobacco, or any nicotine products?"

**If yes:**
> "How long have you been using? Have you tried to quit in the past 12 months?"

**Tobacco rate classes:**
| Status | Classification |
|---|---|
| Non-user (never or quit 12+ months) | Non-tobacco rates |
| Quit less than 12 months ago | Tobacco rates (most carriers) |
| Current user | Tobacco rates |
| Cigar only (1–2/month) | Some carriers allow non-tobacco rates |

### 3. Major Health Conditions

Ask open-endedly first, then probe specifically:

> "Besides [condition already mentioned], have you been diagnosed with or treated for any other major health conditions in the past 10 years?"

Then probe directly for the list below — carriers ask about each of these explicitly on applications.

### 4. Mobility & Daily Living (ADLs)

Carriers often ask about Activities of Daily Living (ADLs). If a prospect needs assistance with bathing, dressing, eating, or transferring, most level carriers will decline.

**How to ask (gently):**
> "Just a couple more questions — do you currently use a walker, wheelchair, or any other assistive device to get around?"

| Status | Impact |
|---|---|
| Independent, no devices | Typically fine |
| Walker (part-time) | Some carriers OK; note on application |
| Walker (full-time) or wheelchair | Usually graded or decline |
| Bedridden or confined to nursing home | Decline or GI only |

### 5. Recent Hospitalizations & Surgeries

**How to ask:**
> "Have you been hospitalized or had any surgeries in the past 2 years — other than outpatient procedures?"

- Hospitalization within 12 months: most carriers require a hold or decline
- Surgery within 6 months: flag for underwriting review
- Outpatient procedures are generally acceptable

## Risk Classification Summary

| Classification | Meaning | Agent Action |
|---|---|---|
| Level / Preferred | Full death benefit from day 1 | Standard application |
| Standard | Full benefit, slightly higher premium | Standard application |
| Graded | Partial benefit years 1–2, full thereafter | Disclose clearly; set expectations |
| Modified | Return of premium years 1–3 + interest | Use only when no other option |
| Guaranteed Issue | Acceptance regardless of health; 2-year wait | Last resort |
| Decline | No coverage available | Refer to GI or other resource |

## How to Deliver Difficult Underwriting News

When a prospect doesn't qualify for level coverage:

> "Based on what you've shared with me, the plan I initially quoted may not be available to you — but I do have another option that a lot of people in your situation use. It works a little differently in the first couple of years, but after that it's the same full coverage. Can I show you how it works?"

Never say "you got declined" — say "that particular plan isn't available" and immediately pivot to what IS available.

## Underwriting Red Flags Checklist

Use during the call to flag potential issues before committing to a product:

- [ ] HIV / AIDS diagnosis
- [ ] Active cancer treatment
- [ ] Dialysis
- [ ] Confined to nursing home or memory care facility
- [ ] Terminal illness diagnosis
- [ ] Cirrhosis or hepatitis C with liver damage
- [ ] Organ transplant recipient
- [ ] ALS (Lou Gehrig's disease)

If any box is checked → move directly to Guaranteed Issue discussion.
