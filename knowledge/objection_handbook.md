# Objection Handling Handbook

## Overview

Every objection is a request for more information or reassurance — not a rejection. This handbook maps the most common final expense objections to proven response frameworks. Memorize the openers; customize the details.

---

## Core Principle: Feel / Felt / Found

Before any specific script, apply the universal bridge:

> "I understand how you **feel**. Many of my clients have **felt** the same way. What they **found** was…"

This disarms defensiveness and positions you as empathetic, not adversarial.

## 1. "I Already Have Insurance"

**What it usually means:** They have some coverage but may be underinsured, or it may be tied to a job or a group plan that could disappear.

- "That's great — what company is it through?"
- "Is it through your employer, or is it something you own personally?"
- "Do you know approximately how much coverage you have?"
- "Is that coverage guaranteed to stay in place no matter what?"

### The Appointment Set

## Objection Stacking

When a prospect raises **multiple objections in sequence**, they are usually recycling the same core hesitation with different words. Identify the real objection:

1. Acknowledge each objection briefly.
2. Ask: "If we could address [objection], is there anything else that would hold you back?"
3. Isolate the real concern before answering.
